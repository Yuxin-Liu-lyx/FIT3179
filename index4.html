Average weekly income in Australia

This is a dashboard of average weekly income in Australia. Designed by Yuxin Liu.




Per capita income by state in Australia

On the right is a map of Australia, with the shades of colour representing the amount of regional income, from light green to dark blue meaning the weekly average per capita income ranges from less to more. Hover over the different state maps to see more detailed information.

This map shows that the Australian Capital Territory is the darkest, followed by Western Australia. This indicates that the ACT is the region with the highest weekly income per capita and WA is the region with the second highest weekly income per capita in Australia.

Average weekly earnings by industry

This bubble chart shows the average weekly earnings of men and women in different industries. The chart divides the 18 industries into four categories, with the categories differentiated by four colours.

The highest paying industry is mining, where the average weekly earnings for both men and women are much higher than the other industries, with men earning an average of $2748.7 and women $2276.8.

Average weekly revenue with public and private

This box plot shows the income of the public and private sectors for men women and per capita respectively. Blue represents public and orange represents private, and by placing the mouse over the colour blocks you can see the highest, lowest and median values of the data.

The graph reflects the fact that men have the highest income levels in the public sector and women have the lowest income levels in the private sector, which reflects, in turn, the fact that women generally earn less than men.
